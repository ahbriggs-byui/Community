Why Does This Matter

Inadequate sleep is prevalent in our communities and across the globe. The struggle to obtain sleep in the evenings is felt at all ages; from little children to college kids, all the way to older adults, the strain is felt everywhere. The need to sleep is programmed into our brains from conception and the way our societies are set up doesnâ€™t always allow for the needed seven hours or more. Inadequate sleep, especially chronic, also has consequences that cannot be overlooked. Inadequate sleep is a silent pandemic across the globe that needs to be addressed, or else the consequences will harm society as a whole.

Inadequate Sleep is associated with:

  • Increased Cardiovascular disease
  • Impaired Memory
  • Weakened Immune System
  • Obesity
